# Frequently asked questions

# Frequently asked questions

* Reference
* 3-min read
* Published Jun 25, 2026

This page answers common questions about the Dynatrace Platform Subscription (DPS) model. For FAQs about a specific DPS capability, see the relevant page at [Capability billing details](/managed/license/capabilities "Understand how Dynatrace calculates and bills consumption for each capability under the Dynatrace Platform Subscription (DPS) model.").

How can I manage my consumption and control my costs?

You get complete transparency into your usage through a suite of tools in the Dynatrace Account Management.
In addition to raw data, the tools provide forecasting, alerting, and drill-downs that give real-time insights into your usage patterns.

Do I get access to all platform capabilities in a Dynatrace Platform Subscription contract?

Yes, the Dynatrace Platform Subscription gives you access to all platform capabilities when you sign the order form.
You can find them on your rate card.

When does Dynatrace charge for overages?

Dynatrace never charges penalty-style overages.
Instead, you can continue to use the platform on an on-demand basis if you consume more than your minimum annual spend commitment, billed monthly at the same rates as pre-paid consumption.
You can alternatively increase your commitment spend to attain a higher discount.

How is monitoring consumption calculated when capabilities are used for less than an hour?

When Dynatrace capabilities are used for a fraction of an hour, we round up to the nearest 15 minutes for billing purposes (for example, one minute of usage is billed as 15 minutes; an hour and three minutes of usage is billed as 1.25 hours).
This billing model is intended to be advantageous in cloud native environments where hosts and services are rapidly spun up and destroyed.

How can I manage who can access consumption information?

You can manage access to consumption information through Dynatrace permissions, see [DPS permissions](/managed/license/dps-permissions "What permissions are required to view DPS consumption data in Account Management, DQL, the API, and Cost Allocation.").

In what timezone are daily usage and cost analysis calculated?

Dynatrace meters usage based on Coordinated Universal Time (UTC).
All times and dates are in UTC+00:00 format.

Daily usage and costs are based on UTC and run from **00:00:00** on the start date of your agreement to **23:59:59** on the end date.

Is the annual commitment period always 12 months? What if I have a three-year subscription?

Multi-year subscriptions are broken down into annual (12-month) subscription periods.

Dynatrace may adjust the subscription period for your agreement to accommodate your needs.
For example, the first period of a multi-year agreement might actually be just a six-month subscription period, which is then followed by two 12-month subscription periods.

What currency is used for Dynatrace Platform Subscription?

Your subscription agreement determines the currency that is used for billing.
The currency code is shown on all Account Management cost views as a three-letter abbreviation (for example, **USD**, **GBP**, or **EUR**).
The applied currency code might not be displayed until after the first cost metrics are calculated.

How can I view my usage from a previous license?

The [View your previous DPS periods via subscription history](/managed/manage-your-costs/reference/subscription-history "View the subscription history of your Dynatrace environment, including usage and cumulative costs for each subscription.") page shows active and expired licenses and subscriptions.
